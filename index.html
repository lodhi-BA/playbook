Delivery Framework

A repeatable four-phase model spanning analytics, requirements, operations, and go-to-market. Each phase maps to a distinct discipline and produces a concrete, measurable output — not just activity.

Phase 01
Discovery & Data Intelligence
Understand & Model
Analytics
Gather and interpret datasets, build statistical models, and identify trends driving demand, cost, or operational risk.
Ops
Audit current-state processes, surface inefficiencies in field operations, inventory, or safety workflows, and quantify the cost of the status quo.
Output A validated baseline — data models, KPI definitions, and a prioritized gap inventory ready to inform requirements.
Phase 02
Requirements & Solution Design
Define & Specify
BA
Translate business and analytical findings into BRDs, SRS, process flows, and UML/SysML models. Define acceptance criteria and regulatory data requirements.
Ops
Document To-Be process designs, PO and budget structures, and data governance rules that the new solution must enforce.
Output Traceable, stakeholder-approved specifications — BRD, SRS, RTM, and process maps — with zero room for misinterpretation.
Phase 03
Operationalization & Delivery
Build & Validate
Analytics
Deploy dashboards, validate models against live data, and confirm reporting pipelines meet regulatory and executive requirements (Power BI, SAP BW, SAS).
Ops
Coordinate UAT, manage capital authorizations and procurement workflows, and verify operational processes against acceptance criteria before go-live.
Output A tested, compliant, production-ready solution — validated by stakeholders and cleared for launch.
Phase 04
GTM, Adoption & Continuous Improvement
Launch & Optimize
GTM
Execute change management and communication plans, deliver role-based training, and support hypercare — ensuring adoption, not just deployment.
Analytics
Monitor KPIs and operational metrics post-launch, identify drift or underperformance, and feed findings back into the next discovery cycle.
Output Sustained adoption and a closed feedback loop — measurable ROI with a clear path to the next improvement cycle.
